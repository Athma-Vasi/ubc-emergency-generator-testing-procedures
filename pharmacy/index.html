Emergency Generator Testing - Pharmacy

***This is not an official Safe Work Procedure***

Testing Procedure

  1. Receive and follow the schedule outlined by the planning coordinator.
  2. Travel to site for generator test.
  3. Contact TELUS (604) 731-4126 and let them know to put the system onto test for the allotted time.

      You will need the following information:

    • Your name
    • Your UBC ID number
    • Pharmacy UN Account Number: UN-7009
  4. Perform a pre-start system inspection on the generator, and associated equipment. Refer to the Emergency Generator Information (EGI) sheets for the building specific information to follow.

    Pharmacy Unit #1 - EGI

    Pharmacy Unit #2 - EGI

    5. Here are some general items to check during the pre-start inspection:

    • Check the fuel level and ensure it is sufficient for the test duration.
    • Inspect the battery and connections for any signs of corrosion or damage.
    • Verify that all control panel indicators are functioning properly. Ensure that there are no warning lights or error messages.
    • Inspect breaker panels and ensure that all breakers are in the correct position.
    • Check the oil level and ensure it is within the recommended range.
    • Check the coolant level and ensure it is within the recommended range.
  6. Shut down any power to the equipment onsite that is affected by the transfer of power.
  7. Complete the pre-check list for generator and fuel tank on the Planon work order.
  8. Before starting the test, verify that there are no alarms or faults in the Fire Alarm Panel.

    Here is the route to the Fire Alarm Panel:

  9. Commence with starting the generators from the transfer switches location.
      Here is the route to to transfer switches location in the electrical room:
    • Go to the basement from the elevator.
    • You will come to Room B102: Electrical Room.
    • There is another door marked B104: Main Building Electrical Room. This is the door to the transfer switches.
  10. There are six ATS (Automatic Transfer Switches) in the room for the two generators. There are two for database servers (ATS-DCA and ATS-DCB). The rest are ATS-E1, ATS-S1, ATS-S2, and ATS-S3. Add more information about the other four ATSs here.
  11. The procedure is the same for each of the ATS'. ATS-DCA is used as an example here.
      The following indicators should be checked on the transfer switches before and after the generator test:
    • Transfer Switch Connected to Normal = Green
    • Normal Source Accepted = Green
      • There must be no red indicators on the transfer switches before and after the generator test. The panel must indicate "NORMAL OK" and "Load On Normal". If there are any faults or alarms, do not proceed! If you are feeling unsafe at any time, do not proceed! Contact your supervisor immediately and follow their instructions.
  12. To begin the generator test, identify the black dial labelled: "Transfer Control". Turn the dial to "Transfer Test", and hold until the panel changes state and indicates: "TD Emerg>Normal: 0:59min59s". The dial should revert back to 12 o-clock position as it is spring loaded. If not, manually revert to 12 o-clock position. The actual time may vary, but the panel should indicate a countdown timer. There may be a loud click. Do not stand in front of the switch. The generator should start and the load should transfer to the generator. Add precautions here.
  13. Here is the state of ATS-DCA (and other switches) during the test:
  14. Repeat the procedure for the rest of the five switches.
  15. Head to generators and ensure that the generator is running.
      Verify the following:
    • The generator is running and producing power.
    • The generator is producing the correct voltage and frequency.
    • The generator is not producing any unusual noises or vibrations.
    • The generator is not producing any smoke or fumes.
    • The generator is not producing any leaks or spills.
    • The generator is not producing any alarms or faults.

  16. Wait for approximately 20 minutes to allow generator state to stabilize. Then fill out appropriate information as required in the Planon work order. Here are some examples of the information that may be required:
    • Generator running time
    • Generator voltage and frequency
    • Generator oil and coolant levels
    • Generator fuel level
    • Generator oil pressure and temperature
  17. After one hour, transfer the load back to the building and turn the generator off. Add details here.
  18. Complete the post-check list for generator and fuel tank on the Planon work order.
  19. Head to the fire alarm panel, acknowledge any troubles or supervisory codes. Reset the fire panel if needed. Perform this action only once, and if the panel does not reset contact FLS for further investigation.
  20. Contact TELUS (604) 731-4126 and let them know to take the system off test.
  21. Record all findings in logbook onsite and let your supervisor know of any issues found.
  22. Fill out the Planon work order and submit for approval.
  23. At the end of shift (or whenever appropriate), record findings in the logbook at the Operations Centre and let your supervisor know of any issues found.
